Severity

Did you know that there are different severity levels of floods?

  • Class 1: large flood events: significant damage to structures or agriculture; fatalities; and/or 1-2 decades-long reported interval since the last similar event.
  • Class 1.5: very large events: greater than 20 yr but less than 100 year recurrence interval, and/or a local recurrence interval of at 10-20 yr.
  • Class 2: Extreme events: with an estimated recurrence interval greater than 100 years

Info source: http://floodobservatory.colorado.edu/Archives/index.html, https://sos.noaa.gov/datasets/flood-events-2000-2009/

    Topography

    Landsat data, like that pictured above, is a remote sensing data product produced by the United States Geological Survey. One basic use of this data is to see topographical changes and weather patterns that cannot be seen at ground level. The images above show different parts of Texas near Corpus Christi during and after Hurrican Harvey. This data can also be used to compare topographical differences (i.e.Hurricane Harvey Shoreline Comparison).
